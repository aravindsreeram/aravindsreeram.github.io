Univariate and Multivariate Regression Models of Survival

To assess the predictive value of a single variable on survival, univariate analyses can be used. In the simplistic model below, I evaluated the impact of four variables (tumor regression grade, nodal stage, and race) on survival. The multivariate regression shows that...

Yet, it is possible that some of these variables are only significant due to their tight correlation with another variable. In this case, we want to identfiy the driver variable which is changing our hazard ratio. Through a multivariate regression model, we can see that only nodal status remains independently predictive, implying that the other two variables were predictive simply due to their association with nodal status.

Kaplan-Meier Survival Curves with Log-Rank Test

The Kaplan-Meier estimator is a common non-parametric statistic employed in survival analysis. In assessing oncologic outcomes, time-to-event data (e.g. whether a death occurs and, if so, when) can be represented through survival curves like the ones below. Here, I am comparing the overall survival of node-positive vs. node-negative gastric cancer patients and employing a logrank test to confirm a statistically significant difference (p < 0.001).
